## Different types of memory

### Semantic
Semantic memories are **memories of facts and general knowledge** that we know but did not personally experience.

**Example**
- Paris is the capital of France.

### Episodic
Episodic memories are **memories of events that have happened in your life** — like episodes of your life.

**Example**
- Your 10th birthday party.

### Procedural
Procedural memories are **memories of how to perform skills or actions**.

**Example**
- How to swim

---

## How memories are encoded and stored

### Encoding, Storage and Retrieval

**Encoding:** The process of converting information into a form that can be stored in memory.  

**Storage:** The process of keeping information in memory for later use.  

**Retrieval:** The process of accessing stored information from memory.

#### Visual Encoding
Encoding information **based on what we see (images)**.

**Example**
- Remembering what a teacher’s books look like.

#### Acoustic Encoding
Encoding information **based on sound**.

**Example**
- Remembering song lyrics.

#### Semantic Encoding
Encoding information based on **meaning**.

**Example**
- Understanding word meanings  
- Understanding concepts or ideas
